Die With Zero

Bill Perkins

Date Read
October 2023
Tags

Buy on Amazon

The Book in 3 Sentences

1. Most people save money for a future they might not be healthy enough to enjoy, optimizing for a number in a bank account rather than life experiences.

2. Using the concept of "memory dividends," investing in experiences early yields a higher return on investment over your lifetime than just compounding interest.

3. The goal of life is to maximize your fulfillment, which means spending your wealth down to zero before you die—otherwise, those hours you worked for that money were wasted.

Impressions

This book completely shifted my perspective on retirement planning and spending. I used to be obsessed with the traditional FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement, which advocates for extreme frugality. Bill Perkins argues effectively that "delaying gratification" can sometimes mean "denying gratification" until it's too late.

It's not about being reckless; it's about being intentional with the resources you have (money, health, time) at the stage of life you are in.

Who Should Read It?

  • Anyone obsessed with saving money but afraid to spend it.
  • Young professionals trying to balance career growth with life experiences.
  • People who want a framework for decision making around money and time.

Top Quotes

"The business of life is the acquisition of memories. In the end that's all there is."
"Delayed gratification in the extreme is the definition of a wasted life. You are saving for a future version of yourself that may never exist."
"Your life is the sum of your experiences. Everything you do—every vacation, every conversation, every project—adds up to who you are."
